Warm, Buttery Lobster Rolls!

Connecticut style lobster rolls on the downtown waterfront. Enjoy the views, maybe catch a speedboat trip while your at it!

American Social Sports Bar on the River

Sports bar along the Miami River. Lots of boat action. You're not rich in Miami unless your yacht has a helicopter on it. Sorry/not sorry.

AWA Sushi Restaurant

Enjoy the ambience of the Miami River. The truffle roll is dee-lish.

Baby Jane Cocktails

$7 happy hour make-your-own-margs until 7pm! Yours truly made a spicy marg made from grapefruit juice, strawberries, tequila, and jalapenos! Across the street from the Brickell W, so yeah, bougie.

Cafe Bastille

Fantastic French brunch spot. Make a reservation or get there early because it gets packed! Nutella chocolate strawberry crepe shown here, drooooool.

Wharf

Sunday funday daytime party atmosphere on the river. Live dj. Took care of the 'tender and he took care of me.

Cajun Boil

Indulge in piles of seafood smothered in cajun sauce. They even have fresh alligator bites and juicy frogs legs.

Myron Mixon

Omg I went here so many times. Southern style hickory/mesquite flavored bbq chicken for lunch, it's tha bomb! Juicy and moist. $19, 2 sides, big enough to share so that price isn't actually that bad -- esp for downtown Miami.

Nusr-Et

Sugar

Wow... best... views... ever... Pay to play though $$$. Need reservations on weekends, expect about a $100 minimum.

Toscana Divino

Perfectly cooked meal. If you're a chef you'll appreciate the attention to detail here. 3 course $25 lunch big enough to share. Right in the middle of Brickell on Miami Ave, great for people watching.

Makoto at Bal Harbor

Sushi. Bougie. Japanese whiskey flight is fab. Also Bal Harbor is where Miami competese with Los Angeleeeez's Bel-Air on bougie-ness, so great for people watching.

Tap 42 in Wynwood

It was actually a challenge to find local made beer in Miami. These guys to the rescue! Fresh local brew. Local, fun crowd.

Fritana Cana Brava & Pinolandia

Where the locals go for homestyle cooking. Big portions, just like momma's house!

Calle Ocho!

THE famous Cuban district of Miami. Tonnes hand rolled cigar shops/lounges

Old's Havana

Awe yeah, get your Cuban on. Enchilado with camarones lunch is only $15 with 2 sides, big enough to share.

La Colada

Coffee shop on Calle 8. Traditional cuban coffee, not the Miami bastardized version. REAL coffee! In-house roasted beans.

Joe's Stone Crab

South Beach bougie and vibin! Closed for 3 months of the year during the Stone Crab offseason. Reservations probably a good idea as it's always packed. Even if you're not dressed for success they have a grab-n-go counter.

Club Deuce

South Beach locals spot. Cash only! Normal prices (not those absurdly high tourist trap prices!) Neat, windy bartop. Little bit of history: Miami Vice tv show writers used to frequent this spot back in the day.

Nikki Beach

Ok I know this is the one ya insta-whores have been waiting for ya filthy animals! But seriously, it's pretty chill. Saturdays channel your inner white girl wastedness with $25 bottles rose.

Seaquarium

Freee Willyyyyyyyyy! Runs on weekends. Get there early and you'll be all done after 12:40pm killer whale (oops I mean orca but you might get lucky and he'll go crazy and eat someone) show before the lines start getting long.

Hooters! at Coral Gables

I know you're just here for the wings but seriously the snow crab is a good deal and as far as Miami goes the drink prices are some of the best around. Fun vibe, chill locals.

Versailles

Good and surprisingly inexpensive Cuban cafe/bakery/restaurant (it has like 3 parts to it) that every tourist feels they must go to. Hey I don't make the rules.

Carpaccio in Bal Harbor

Wow, another spot of culinary excellence. If you're a foody you'll devine in the gastronomic artistry of their sichuan orange carpaccio.
